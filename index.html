Equity & Exposure 05 / 08

Who Bears the Risk

Higher Exposure

Clerical & Administrative Workers Data entry, scheduling, bookkeeping — highest automation overlap
Women (2× more exposed than men) Concentrated in white-collar clerical roles most susceptible to LLM automation
Low-income country workers Only 0.4% AI exposure vs. 5.5% in high-income countries — but limited safety nets
Younger workers (<35) Entry-level roles most at risk; career ladders being compressed

Better Positioned

Managers & Senior Professionals AI as productivity multiplier; strategic judgment remains scarce
High-income / capital-owning workers Complementarity effect: AI amplifies existing skill premiums + capital gains
Workers in digitally advanced economies Institutional readiness, retraining infrastructure, and AI-complementary industries
STEM & creative knowledge workers AI augments output quality without replacing creative or scientific judgment

Sources: ILO Working Paper 96 · IMF Working Paper 2025/068
